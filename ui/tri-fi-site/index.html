The problem: survivors hidden under rubble

In large‑scale disasters, victims are often trapped beneath layers of concrete, steel and debris. Visibility is close to zero, structures are unstable, and every minute lost reduces the chance of survival.

Short rescue window

Disaster agencies report that over 90% of survivors are rescued within the first 24 hours—after that, survival rates drop sharply.

Limited sensing

Cameras and thermal sensors struggle through dense rubble, dust and reinforced concrete, leaving large blind spots underground.

Slow, manual searches

Rescue dogs and manual sweeps can take hours to cover a single collapsed building, delaying extraction when time matters most.

High‑cost radars

Advanced radar life‑detection units cost tens of thousands of dollars, limiting access for many response teams.

Our solution: TRI‑FI portable RF survivor scanner

TRI‑FI turns commodity Wi‑Fi into a low‑cost, field‑deployable survivor detection system. ESP32‑S3 nodes form a triangular RF mesh that senses subtle disturbances in the 2.4 GHz channel caused by human bodies—through non‑metallic rubble and debris.

  • 10–15 m scan radius through rubble within each triangular unit.
  • Battery powered for up to 24 hours of continuous operation.
  • Sub‑second AI analysis converts RF patterns into survivor probability.
  • Real‑time heatmap dashboard guides teams to the highest‑priority zones.
RF‑based human detection CSI / RSSI changes reveal motion and breathing behind debris.
AI‑powered signal analysis Models distinguish survivors from environmental noise.
Multi‑node consensus Triangular fusion reduces false positives and edge artefacts.

Technical approach

Hardware

  • ESP32‑S3 microcontrollers as IoT edge nodes
  • 2.4 GHz RF transmit/receive antennas
  • Dual receiver configuration for richer CSI
  • 5 V portable power bank (Li‑ion) supply
  • Wi‑Fi / ESP‑NOW wireless backhaul

Signal & AI stack

  • Python‑based RF signal preprocessing
  • RSSI / CSI time‑series feature extraction
  • Machine‑learning models for presence vs noise
  • Breathing and micro‑motion analysis (sign‑of‑life)

Visualization & comms

  • Web‑based survivor probability heatmap
  • Real‑time dashboard for NDRF / SDRF teams
  • Wi‑Fi IoT communication protocols
  • Duty‑cycled operation for extended battery life

Prototype: from lab to rubble‑ready

Our current prototype uses three ESP32‑S3 nodes in a triangular formation, powered by portable banks and connected to the TRI‑FI dashboard.

  • Deployment time under one minute with 8‑second calibration.
  • 10–15 m scan radius per triangle, extendable with more nodes.
  • Live survivor heatmap and node health telemetry.

Impact & benefits

Social impact

Faster, data‑driven detection of survivors trapped under rubble improves rescue speed, reduces missed victims and increases survival probabilities.

Economic impact

Offers a low‑cost, scalable alternative to radar systems—reducing equipment spend and improving operational efficiency for agencies and NGOs.

Environmental impact

Focused excavation on high‑probability zones reduces unnecessary demolition, energy use and secondary damage to already fragile structures.

Target users

  • NDRF / SDRF and state disaster response forces
  • Military search‑and‑rescue units
  • International humanitarian and relief organisations

Positioning

TRI‑FI is designed as a field‑ready, RF‑based alternative to high‑end radar life‑detection systems—compact enough for backpacks, powerful enough for real deployments.

Roadmap & future scope

TRI‑FI is built to evolve with real‑world deployments and deeper research into RF sensing for disaster response.

Technical roadmap

  • Increase number of nodes for large‑area coverage.
  • Improve multi‑person detection and crowd scenarios.
  • Add depth estimation to infer how deep a survivor is buried.
  • Enhance robustness in noisy RF environments.

Deployment roadmap

  • Integrate with drones and ground robots for remote scans.
  • Field trials with NDRF, SDRF, DRDO and state agencies.
  • Government funding via TDF, PFP‑TIFAC and innovation grants.

Research pipeline

  • RF‑based human detection using Wi‑Fi CSI disturbances.
  • Through‑wall sensing for collapsed‑structure environments.
  • AI models for breathing and micro‑motion "sign‑of‑life" analysis.
